1.1 Organisation Description:  

Durban Connect was started in 2026 by a first-year student with an interest in technology and community development. The idea came from the need for a better way to find and keep up with local events happening around Durban. 

Durban Connect is a digital platform designed for the Durban community. It also allows users to search for events by date, category, or location, and provides clear, up-to-date information such as venues, ticket details, and event updates. Users can also RSVP directly on the platform, making it easier to plan ahead and keep track of events they are interested in. 

For event organisers, Durban Connect offers a simple way to promote events and reach a wider audience. Instead of relying only on social media or word of mouth, organisers can list their events in one central place. The platform supports all types of events, including markets, live shows, workshops, festivals, and community gatherings. 

What makes Durban Connect different is its focus on community. Users can connect with friends, share event plans, and see what others are attending (with permission). These features make the experience more interactive and help people feel more connected to what’s happening around them. 

Durban Connect also aims to support local culture and small businesses by making events more visible and accessible. By bringing everything into one platform, it helps more people discover and take part in local activities. 

Although still in its early stages, Durban Connect continues to grow and improve. The long-term goal is to become the go-to platform for discovering events in Durban, for residents, students, visitors, and tourists alike.  

Reasons for Choosing the Organisation:  

The platform was created because many people in Durban struggle to find reliable and up-to-date information about local events. The idea came from a personal experience in late 2025. After planning for weeks to attend an event, it was missed due to poor communication. Important details like the venue, ticket availability, and updates were difficult to find. By the time the correct information was shared through informal channels, the event had already sold out. Similar situations happened with markets, live performances, and community gatherings. 

At the time, event details were spread across WhatsApp groups, social media pages, and word of mouth. This made it hard for students, newcomers, and tourists to stay informed. Event organisers also struggled to reach a wider audience, as they mostly relied on limited promotion.

3.Current Website Analysis 

The current Durban Connect website provides a solid foundation for helping users discover and engage with events in Durban. However, there are several strengths that support its purpose, as well as areas that can be improved to enhance user experience, functionality, and overall effectiveness. 

2.1 Strengths of Website 

Simple and User-Friendly Structure — The website layout is easy to understand and navigate, making it accessible to a wide range of users. 

Clear Purpose and Focus — The platform is dedicated to event discovery and community engagement within Durban, giving it a clear objective. 

Event Categorisation — Events are organised into categories such as Music, Festivals, and Community events, making browsing easier. 

Event Submission Feature — Users and event organisers can submit events through a dedicated submission page. 

RSVP Functionality — Users can indicate attendance for events, improving engagement and event planning. 

Community Interaction Features — The website includes user connection features such as following users, sharing events, and viewing activity. 

Dedicated Information Pages — Pages such as About Us and Contact & Support provide users with relevant information and assistance. 

Responsive Navigation Structure — The sitemap is logically organised, allowing users to move between pages efficiently. 

3.2 Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement 

Limited Search Functionality — The current design lacks advanced search and filtering options for finding events quickly. 

No Personalised Recommendations — Users do not receive event suggestions based on interests or previous activity. 

Limited Event Details — Event pages could provide more comprehensive information such as maps, organiser profiles, and ticketing links. 

No Online Ticket Purchasing Integration — The website does not currently support direct event ticket purchases. 

Limited Social Media Integration — Event sharing and promotion through external social media platforms can be expanded. 

Lack of User Reviews and Ratings — Users cannot rate or review events, limiting feedback and credibility. 

No Event Reminder System — The platform does not provide email or notification reminders for upcoming events. 

Limited User Profile Features — User accounts could be enhanced with saved events, favourites, and event history. 

No Mobile Application Support — The platform is currently web-based and lacks a dedicated mobile application. 

Basic Community Features — Community engagement could be improved through discussion forums, messaging, and event groups. 

Limited Analytics for Organisers — Event organisers do not have access to detailed attendance and engagement statistics. 

Potential Scalability Challenges — As the number of users and events increases, performance optimisation and database management may become necessary.

Website Pages and Content  

 

  

Home 

The Home page of Durban Connect is the main page that users see first. It is designed to be simple, modern, and easy to use. The colours used are bright and attractive to match the fun and energetic feeling of events and entertainment in Durban. 

The page includes the Durban Connect logo at the top, which represents the platform, and a slogan “Where the vibe is at” to attract users and show the purpose of the website.
